People with ALS experience a major change in their lives; they lose the ability to move, speak, eat, and breathe, but their minds stay completely conscious. After the diagnosis, life expectancy ranges from 3 to 5 years, and there is no known cure for ALS. Symptoms begin with weakness in arms and legs, muscle twitching, slurred speech, and difficulty chewing or swallowing. Later into the diagnosis there are other symptoms like mostly paralyzed muscles and having trouble breathing and eating and drinking. While again there is no cure, researchers are working to find and develop new treatments. 4 drugs that have helped the disease, like Qualsandy. Qualsandy treats ALS brought on by a mutation in the SOD1 gene by preventing toxicity. It is a drug that blocks harmful SOD1 proteins that hurt the nerve cells as the protein builds up and causes muscle weakness. It slows down the disease from getting worse. There is also Radicava and Riluzole that help slow down the diseases; these are different from Qualsandy because it is more of a gene-related drug, while the others are more general. Nuedexta is the other drug that helps control sudden episodes with ALS, making emotions easier to control.
